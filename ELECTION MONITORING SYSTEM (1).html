First-Time Voter Guide

Everything you need to know to cast your vote with confidence

1

Check Registration

Verify your voter registration status at least 2 weeks before election day. You can check online or at your local election office.

  • Have your ID ready
  • Note your polling station
  • Save your voter ID number
2

Know Your Candidates

Research all candidates and parties on your ballot. Understand their policies, track records, and manifestos.

  • Read official manifestos
  • Watch debates
  • Check verified fact sources
3

Cast Your Vote

On election day, bring required ID, go to your assigned polling station, and follow the voting procedure.

  • Arrive during less busy hours
  • Keep your vote secret
  • Verify your vote registered

Frequently Asked Questions

You need a valid government-issued photo ID such as Voter ID card (EPIC), Passport, Driving License, Aadhaar Card, or PAN Card. Some polling stations may accept other documents - check with your local election office.
Don't panic! You can request a "Tendered Ballot" at the polling station. The presiding officer will verify your identity and may allow you to cast a provisional vote. You should also file a complaint with the election commission.
Generally, you must vote at your registered polling station. However, some elections offer postal ballots for specific categories like service voters, senior citizens (80+), or persons with disabilities. Check if you qualify for remote voting options.
You can use our "Report an Issue" feature on this platform, contact the Election Commission's helpline, or speak directly to election observers at your polling station. Document everything you observe and report promptly.
